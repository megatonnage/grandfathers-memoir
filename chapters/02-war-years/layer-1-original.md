If You’re Still Trying to ‘Hit Harder,’ Congratulations, You’re Playing Pickleball on Easy Mode
Most players think they’re losing because they’re not strong enough…but the real reason is they’re not thinking enough.
Trust me, I get it. Smacking the ball as hard as possible is fun. It feels primal. It feels heroic. For a moment, you’re Thor with a paddle.
then you miss.Or they block it back.Or you hit your partner.(We’ve all been there. Some of us more than once. Sorry, Dave.)
The best players in the world don’t win by overpowering people.They win by outmaneuvering them.
If you want to jump a full skill tier without adding a single MPH to your shots, out-think the people who keep out hitting you.
1. Stop Aiming for Winners, Aim for Problems
Recreational players love aiming for highlight shots.Smart players aim for inconvenience.They’re not trying to finish the rally; they’re trying to create chaos:Balls at anklesBalls to the paddle side hipBalls that force a reachBalls that force a resetBalls that make the opponent do something they don’t want to doYour opponent doesn’t need to be beaten.They need to be pressured.Great pickleball isn’t about winning shots. It’s about creating uncomfortable situations consistently.
2. Stop Playing “Your Game”, Start Playing Theirs (Against Them)
Every opponent has a pattern and a preference.Some love their forehand.Some panic on the backhand.Some can’t handle low balls.Some crumble the moment anything speeds up.Your job isn’t to play your A game blindly.Your job is to weaponize their C game.This is how smart players win without having to be spectacular.Think chess, not arm wrestling.Identify your opponent’s favorite shot, then relentlessly deny it.
3. Slow Down the Rally to Speed Up Their Mistakes
Most players beat themselves, if you just stop helping them.Everyone wants to hit faster, harder, louder.Great players are annoyingly calm.They slow the tempo. They reset chaos. They force opponents to generate their own pace.Most opponents collapse trying.It’s strategic suffocation.A reset isn’t surrender. It’s a trap.
4. Use “Predictability Punishment”
The average player is predictable:They speed up the same wayThey lob the same wayThey panic the same wayThey dink the same directionThey telegraph shots with body language that might as well be closed captioningSmart players watch, and punish.If you know they speed up from the right hip every time?Slide left an inch. Counter. Watch their soul leave their body.If you can predict it, you can punish it.
